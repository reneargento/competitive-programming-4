6.3.1.1

The cost of a match is +2 and the costs of replace, insert and delete are all -1 for the algorithm to prioritize the matches due to their higher weight.
They are not magic numbers and the costs for replace, insert and delete can be different, as different scoring schemes lead to different global alignments.
A +1 score for match would also work for string alignment, the only difference is that the algorithm becomes less eager to align characters.
This means that sometimes several different alignments may end up with the same optimal score, whereas under +2 one of them would be preferred.
